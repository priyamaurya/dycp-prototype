Penalties, convictions and court rulings

Tell us if you or a connected person has received any penalties, convictions or court rulings that may exclude you from the procurement process.

If someone other than yourself committed the offence, make sure you add them as a connected person (opens in new tab).

You must tell us if the offence was in the UK, who it applies to, their email address and upload any supporting documents you may have. If any non-UK offences apply, tell us the equivalent offence.

Penalties

This includes HMRC findings for deliberate VAT, tax, duty and excise wrongdoing such as fraud, evasion, abusive practice, failure to notify, defeat in respect of notifiable tax arrangements and failure to cooperate with investigation. Also, any Competition and Markets Authority decisions for competition law infringement.

Convictions

This includes corporate manslaughter, terrorism, theft, fraud and bribery, labour market, slavery and human trafficking, organised crime, tax offences, cartel offences and aiding, abetting and conspiring to commit offences.

Penalties and convictions are detailed in Schedule 6 of the Procurement Act 2023 (opens in new tab).

Court rulings

This includes labour market, environmental and professional misconduct, insolvency and bankruptcy, suspending or ceasing to carry on business, competition law infringements, breach of contract and acting improperly in procurement.

Court rulings and misconducts are detailed in Schedule 7 of the Procurement Act 2023 (opens in new tab).
