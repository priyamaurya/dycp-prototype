Prepare to complete your supplier information

What supplier information is

Suppliers must provide contracting authorities with certain information under the Procurement Act 2024 if they want to bid on a public procurement.

Under previous legislation suppliers provided information by completing parts 1 and 2 of the Supplier Selection Questionnaire (PPN 8/16).

The supplier information section of this service replaces the supplier questionnaire. It allows suppliers to fill out the required information and create a share code to supply to eSenders when bidding on a procurement.

When to update your supplier information

You’ll be asked for your supplier information, often in the form of a share code, when bidding for public procurement.

Your supplier information is saved in this service. You only need to update it when information changes.

If you've created a share code and then go on to update your supplier information, you’ll need to create a new share code.

When your information is saved

You don’t have to enter your supplier information in one go but your information is only saved when you click a ‘save’ button.

In some sections, you can save after one or 2 pages. In other sections, it may be 10 or more pages before you can save your information.

If you start a section but haven’t clicked the ‘save’ button, your information will be lost if you:

  • close your browser
  • navigate away from the service
  • leave the page open for more than an hour

The information you need for each section

Your organistion

Each of these sections can be saved independently of the ‘Your organisation’ section. You can complete one and save it and then come back to complete another.

Where a question isn’t relevant, you need to answer ‘no’ to complete the section.

  • The name of your organisation or the name you trade under
  • Your organisation’s registered address
  • Your business postal address, if different or if you aren’t a registered company
  • A registry number if you have one, for example a Companies House number or a Charity number
  • Your VAT number, if you have one
  • If your organisation is a:
    • small or medium-sized enterprise (SME)
    • voluntary, community and social enterprise (VCSE)
    • supported employment provider
    • public service mutual
  • The legal formation of your company and the law that defines it. For example, a private limited company under the Companies Act 2006.

People connected to your organisation

A 'connected person' is someone who influences or controls your company. It includes ‘people of significant control’ and any other individual, group or company that has a declarable interest in your business.

For example, a director, a trust, a majority shareholder or a parent or subsidiary company.

You need to add one connected person at a time. The information you complete will depend on who the ‘person’ is and how they’re connected to your organisation.

For organisations you need:

  • The name of the connected organisation
  • Its registered address
  • Its business postal address, if different
  • Its company number if registered with Companies House, or non-UK equivalent
  • How the organisation is connected to yours. For example, owns shares or has voting rights.
